# Ethics Position

## Ethical Purpose

LORI-DICE is grounded in the belief that responsible AI reasoning should account for ambiguity without treating all interpretations as equally safe, useful, or appropriate.

The framework is intended to support public discussion about how AI systems might reason across multiple possible meanings while still converging toward human-context-aligned answers.

## Interpretive Responsibility

When a prompt has more than one valid meaning, an AI system should not simply choose randomly, maximize novelty, or produce every possible answer without judgment.

It should consider whether an interpretation is:

- Useful to the user's likely intent
- Consistent with available context
- Clear enough to answer responsibly
- Safe enough to act on
- Respectful of human agency and consequences

This does not require exposing private scoring methods. It requires a public ethical commitment to caution, clarity, and contextual awareness.

## Convergence and Human Context

Convergence is an ethical act as well as a reasoning act.

When an AI system chooses one interpretation as primary, it shapes what the user sees, understands, and may act upon. For that reason, convergence should be guided by context, responsibility, and humility.

In low-risk cases, the system may answer directly. In ambiguous cases, it may acknowledge multiple readings. In high-risk cases, it may need to ask clarifying questions or avoid giving instructions that could cause harm.

## Avoiding Overclaiming

This public framework should not be described as:

- A completed algorithm
- A guaranteed safety mechanism
- A substitute for human judgment
- A full implementation of ethical reasoning
- A disclosure of proprietary internal logic

It is a conceptual framework for thinking about responsible multi-interpretation reasoning.

## Public and Private Boundary

The public version of LORI-DICE may describe principles, examples, and ethical commitments.

It must not include:

- Proprietary scoring formulas
- Internal decision weights
- Private risk-ranking logic
- Operational safety thresholds
- Hidden evaluation procedures
- Implementation details

This boundary protects both intellectual property and responsible system design.

## Ethical Commitment

LORI-DICE centers a simple principle:

AI should not only think differently; it should know when a different interpretation is useful, when it is risky, and when it must converge toward a human-context-aligned answer.
